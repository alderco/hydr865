Recently, there have been advances in weight-loss assistance in the form of semaglutide and tirzepatide, many may know them by the brand names Ozempic and Manjaro.

Originally, the medications were prescribed to individuals with Type-2 diabetes. The medical field noticed one of the side effects was controlled weight loss. In 2021, the FDA approved semaglutide to assist in weight-loss efforts and in May of 2022, tirzepatide was approved by the FDA to combat obesity and assist with an individual’s weight loss journey.

What is the difference in the two?

Healthline.com has a great explanation, “Semaglutide is FDA approved to treat type 2 diabetes under the brand names Ozempic, Wegovy, and Rybelsus. Semaglutide is a glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonist, which means it mimics the GLP-1 hormone and makes you feel less hungry. When you eat, your digestive tract releases the GLP-1 hormone, prompting the body to create more insulin. It can also send fullness signals to the brain. Tirzepatide is sold under the brand name Mounjaro. It’s both a glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP) and GLP-1 receptor agonist. Tirzepatide mimics the GLP-1 hormone and the GIP hormone. The GIP hormone can also trigger insulin creation and sensations of fullness. This means tirzepatide may offer an extra weight loss boost.”
